# Revised execution plan

This execution plan uses dual sign-off from Phase 1 onward:

- **Definition delivered** - the decision/spec/checklist exists and is locked
- **Implementation delivered** - the thing exists in the product or workflow
- **Verified** - it works in a real or realistic scenario
- **Signed off** - it is accepted and moved forward without casual reopening

- Implementation delivered: yes

- Signed off: yes

### Phase 1

## Phase 2 - Build the trust layer

Exit criteria:

- workspace isolation is implemented
- credentials are brand-scoped
- author/account resolution is validated
- pre-publish validation blocks bad jobs
- audit logging is end-to-end
- retries are safe and idempotent
- no cross-brand publishing risk remains

## Phase 3 - Make generation useful

Exit criteria:

- brand context packs exist per workspace
- objectives are explicit and reusable
- generation returns structured outputs
- reviewers can consistently approve/reject
- a meaningful percentage of drafts are usable with light edits

## Phase 4 - Build the minimum usable product

Exit criteria:

- onboarding exists
- asset upload exists
- draft review exists
- schedule/status views exist
- admin/operator console exists
- a new workspace can move from intake to scheduled publishing

## Phase 5 - Sell the launch offer

Exit criteria:

- one-page offer is live
- prospect list exists
- outreach is being sent
- discovery calls are logged
- at least two customers pay

## Phase 6 - Deliver the launch plan

Exit criteria:

- a real customer completes the full workflow
- friction points are logged
- value metrics are captured
- continuation or renewal looks plausible

## Phase 7 - Measure success

Core metrics:

- onboarding completion rate
- draft usability rate
- approval speed
- publish success rate
- cross-brand incident count
- month-two retention
- willingness to expand

## Phase 8 - Strengthen the wedge

Improve quality, approvals, onboarding, and billing before expanding scope.

## Phase 9 - Expand carefully

Do not expand to other platforms or ICPs until the LinkedIn wedge is paid, retained, and operationally reliable.
