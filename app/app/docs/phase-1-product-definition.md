# Phase 1 - Product definition

## User journey

1. Create workspace
2. Connect LinkedIn account
3. Add brand and client details
4. Upload assets and examples
5. Choose the content objective
6. Generate draft posts
7. Review and edit drafts
8. Approve drafts
9. Schedule posts
10. Publish posts
11. Review logs and publishing status

## Required inputs

A new customer must be able to provide:

- brand name
- industry
- offer or service
- target audience
- tone of voice
- goals
- CTA preferences
- banned topics or claims
- website/profile URLs
- example posts
- approved assets
- cadence
- campaign dates

## Output schema

Each generated post should be a structured object with:

- platform
- workspace ID
- campaign theme
- objective
- hook
- caption
- CTA
- asset references
- approval status
- scheduled date/time
- publishing status
- audit metadata

## Good content definition

A draft is considered good when it is:

- on-brand
- objective-aligned
- specific rather than generic
- safe and non-risky
- platform-appropriate
- publishable with light editing

## Review rule

Approvals and rejections must use clear reasons instead of vague taste-based feedback.
