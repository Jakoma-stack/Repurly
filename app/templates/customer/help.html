Use this guide when you want a plain-English explanation of how Repurly works. It covers onboarding, brand setup, asset uploads, content drafting, scheduling and what each publishing format means.

Launch checklist

  1. Check your workspace name, billing contact and allowed domains in Settings.
  2. Add at least one brand in Brands.
  3. Upload your logo and starter images in Assets.
  4. Create drafts, choose the right format, generate AI-assisted copy, and schedule them from Content & schedule.

Choosing the right format

Text only Use this when the copy carries the idea on its own. Leave the asset picker empty.
Single image Use this when one graphic, logo, screenshot or photo supports the post. Select exactly one uploaded asset.
Carousel Use this when the story is better spread across multiple slides or screenshots. Select at least two assets. If live carousel publishing is not enabled yet for your environment, save or submit the post for review rather than self-scheduling it.

Review and scheduling

  1. Save draft if you are still writing or collecting assets.
  2. Submit for review if you want the post checked before it is allowed into the publishing pipeline.
  3. Schedule for publishing if the draft is ready and you want Repurly to treat it as approved.
Scheduling creates the dated content package used by the publishing pipeline: caption file, asset files, schedule row, and customer-facing status updates.

AI-assisted generation

  1. Use the AI-assisted generator in Content & schedule when you want Repurly to draft copy from your brand tone, audience, CTA and brief.
  2. Choose Create drafts only if you want to edit the copy before it enters the queue.
  3. Choose Create and schedule automatically if you want a ready-made content plan. You can now choose daily, weekdays, weekly, or 3x-weekly cadences plus same-time, staggered, or random workday timing.
Bulk weekly plans work best when your brand settings and CTA fields are already filled in. If the copy is close but not perfect, edit the generated draft before scheduling it.

Status guide

StatusMeaning
draftThe post exists in your workspace but is not in the schedule yet.
pendingThe post has been submitted for review and is waiting for approval.
approvedThe post is cleared for publishing and can move through the pipeline.
postedThe platform accepted the post and a publish record exists.
failedThe publish attempt failed and needs review or retry.

What to do if something looks wrong

  • If a text post shows an image error, clear the asset picker or switch the format.
  • If a single-image post is rejected, check that exactly one asset is selected and still exists in Assets.
  • If a scheduled post is missing from the queue, open Content & schedule and confirm you used Submit for review or Schedule for publishing rather than only Save draft.
  • If billing or login behave unexpectedly, use Billing or email {{ support_email }}.
