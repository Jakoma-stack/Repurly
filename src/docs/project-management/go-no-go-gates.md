# Go or no-go gates

## Goal of these gates

Do not confuse product progress with business validation.

## Product-ready gates

All of these should be true:
- LinkedIn connect flow works in a real environment
- post creation and editing work
- target selection works
- approval flow works
- schedule flow works
- job detail shows real data
- retry or reconnect guidance exists
- obvious placeholders are removed from launch paths

## Pilot-ready gates

At least these should be true:
- demo can be run end to end
- onboarding checklist exists
- pricing sheet exists
- support flow is defined
- one pilot can be delivered manually if needed

## Commercial validation gates

Continue investing aggressively only if at least most of these become true:
- 10 to 15 discovery calls completed
- at least 3 paid pilots
- at least 2 customers complete onboarding and use the workflow
- at least 1 customer strongly prefers workflow and reliability over channel breadth
- the intended price band does not trigger universal pushback

## Stop or rethink signals

Pause or narrow further if:
- buyers mainly ask for broad multi-platform support
- pricing gets rejected without clear value pull
- pilot users do not care about approvals or reliability
- onboarding and support load is far too manual for the price point
- the product still sells only on founder persuasion rather than clear pain
