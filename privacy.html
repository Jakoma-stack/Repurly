Privacy notice

Repurly privacy notice

This notice explains how Repurly handles personal data collected through the marketing site, signup flow, billing flow, onboarding flow, support communications and customer workspace. Repurly is built and operated by Jakoma.

Main contact: support@repurly.org. This notice is a practical launch-ready baseline and should still be reviewed against your final legal entity, jurisdiction and live providers before broader scale-up.

1. Who is responsible for your data

Repurly is the service and public-facing brand. The product is built and operated by Jakoma. Until replaced with your final trading entity details, privacy enquiries may be sent to support@repurly.org.

2. Data we may collect

  • Identity and contact details, such as name, work email, company name, company website and business enquiry details.
  • Commercial and billing data, including selected plan, billing status, Stripe checkout and subscription references, billing contact details and cancellation-related records.
  • Workspace and onboarding data, such as passwords in hashed form, workspace settings, team invitations, brand information, logos, uploaded assets, scheduling preferences and support-related setup notes.
  • Content workflow data, including draft text, review notes, approval states, post schedules, publish attempt results and audit history.
  • Technical and security data, such as IP address, browser information, device information, request logs, error logs and authentication events.
  • Referral and attribution data carried through URL parameters such as UTM source, medium, campaign and content values.

3. Why we use this data

  • To operate, secure and improve the website, signup flow, billing flow and customer workspace.
  • To create and administer customer accounts, process commercial enquiries, manage subscriptions and provide onboarding support.
  • To support brand setup, asset storage, drafting, scheduling, publishing operations and operational oversight.
  • To investigate failures, maintain audit records, review product issues and prevent misuse or fraud.
  • To communicate with customers about support, billing, service changes and important product updates.
  • To understand which campaigns and traffic sources are driving signups and commercially relevant activity.

4. Lawful bases

Depending on the context, Repurly may rely on one or more of the following lawful bases:

  • Contract or steps prior to entering a contract, where you request access, start checkout, become a paying customer, or use the product.
  • Legitimate interests for business operations, product improvement, platform security, support, fraud prevention, campaign measurement and operational record-keeping.
  • Consent where consent is required, such as optional launch updates or any future non-essential cookies or marketing tools.
  • Legal obligation where records must be kept for tax, accounting, security or regulatory reasons.

5. Payments and billing providers

Repurly may use third-party payment and billing providers, including Stripe, to process checkout, manage subscriptions and provide billing portal functionality. Repurly does not need to store full payment card details directly if a payment provider handles those details on its secure systems. Billing-related events, subscription status, invoice references and similar commercial metadata may still be stored inside Repurly systems for account administration and support.

6. Customer content and uploaded assets

When customers use the product, Repurly may process brand assets, logos, uploaded images, draft content, scheduling details, review notes and operational metadata required to deliver the service. Customers are responsible for ensuring they have the necessary rights and permissions for content they upload, store, schedule or publish through the platform.

7. Cookies and similar technologies

The public marketing site is intended to operate without non-essential cookies or advertising trackers by default. If analytics tools, remarketing tools, live chat services or embedded third-party services requiring consent are added later, this notice and any required consent mechanism should be updated before those tools are activated.

8. Sharing data

Repurly does not sell personal data. Data may be shared with service providers that help host, secure, support, email, authenticate, bill, monitor or operate the service where reasonably necessary and subject to appropriate safeguards. Data may also be disclosed where required by law, to enforce rights, or to investigate suspected misuse, fraud or security issues.

9. International transfers

Some suppliers or infrastructure providers may process data outside your home jurisdiction. Where required, Repurly aims to use appropriate safeguards for international transfers, such as adequacy mechanisms or standard contractual clauses.

10. Retention

Repurly keeps personal data only for as long as reasonably necessary for service delivery, support, billing, security, legal compliance, audit review and product improvement. Retention periods may vary depending on account status, contractual requirements, payment records, support history and operational need.

11. Security

Repurly aims to use reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect personal data, including access controls, authentication, secure payment processing through third-party providers, and operational review of logs and system health. No internet-based service can guarantee absolute security, so customers should also use strong passwords, protect their credentials and notify Repurly promptly if they suspect unauthorised access.

12. Your rights

Depending on applicable law, you may have rights to request access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection and, in some cases, portability. You may also have rights relating to marketing preferences and the right to complain to a supervisory authority.

13. Contact

For privacy questions, data requests, or concerns about how Repurly handles personal data, contact support@repurly.org.

14. Updates

This notice may be updated from time to time to reflect product changes, provider changes, legal requirements or operational improvements. The latest version published here will apply from the stated publication date unless another date is specified.
