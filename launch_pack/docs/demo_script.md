# Repurly Demo Script

## Demo goal
Show that Repurly removes the friction between drafting, approving, and publishing.
Do not over-explain the product. Show the workflow.

## Demo length
8 to 10 minutes.

## Before the call
- Have one polished demo workspace ready
- Use realistic brand names and content examples
- Make sure statuses are visible: draft, in review, approved, scheduled, published
- Have one mock failed post and one recovered post ready to show reliability

## Demo flow
### 1. Open with the problem
"Most teams do not actually have a content problem. They have a workflow problem. Content gets stuck between draft, approval, and publishing."

### 2. Show the main dashboard
Narrate the workflow in one sentence:
"This is the control centre where your team can see what is drafted, what is waiting for approval, and what is scheduled to publish."

### 3. Show creation
Create or open a draft and explain:
"Everything starts here. Your team writes or generates content in one place instead of bouncing between documents, Slack, and email."

### 4. Show approval
Move the draft into review.
"This is where comments, edits, and approvals happen without messy back-and-forth."

### 5. Show scheduling and publish status
"Once approved, you can schedule confidently and still keep visibility on what is going live."

### 6. Show multi-brand or team capability
"For agencies and multi-brand teams, this is where the system becomes valuable because every brand stays organised under one workflow."

### 7. Show reliability and visibility
"You can see what is live, what failed, and what needs attention, which is where most manual systems fall apart."

## Close script
"Usually at this point teams do one of two things: either they try to stitch this together themselves, or they have us set it up properly so it works from day one. Most choose the second option because it saves weeks of friction."

## Price anchor
"That usually looks like a setup fee plus a monthly plan depending on brands, users, and support."

## Final close question
"Does that sound like something you would want help implementing?"

## Objection handling prompt
If they hesitate, ask:
"What would stop this working for you?"
